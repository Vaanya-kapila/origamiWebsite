Origami Designs

Word origami originates from two words ori meaning “folding” and kami meaning “paper.”
These two words, origami can be defined as the practice of folding paper.
Origami is mostly associated with the Japanese culture, although its origins can also be traced to some parts of Europe and China.
The traditional art of origami focuses on folding paper without modifying it in any way, either by cutting, taping, marking, or gluing.
Although origami can take on many designs, the more popular model includes the Japanese paper crane.

Intersting Facts About Camel

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camel can move easily across the sand because of its specially designed feet.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.
  • Camels are very strong and can carry up to 900 pounds for 25 miles a day.

Intersting Facts About Chameleon

  • Chameleons actually change their colour due to a number of different factors.
  • Chameleons’ eyesight has developed both to help them catch prey and escaping from predators.
  • They can move their eyes separately, with each eye having a field of 180 degrees.
  • They can focus their eyes relatively well, meaning they can judge distances and spot prey between 5 and 10 metres..

Intersting Facts About Pigeon

  • Pigeons have fought alongside humans in World War I and II.
  • Pigeons can inbreed exactly up to 21 times before the effects become detrimental.
  • Pigeons always lay at least 2 eggs with each one being a male and a female.
  • Pigeons are an invasive species, shrinking the population of smaller birds when theirs increases.

Intersting Facts About Panda

  • Panda have great camouflage for their environment.
  • For the 1% of their diet that isn't bamboo, pandas eat eggs, small animals, carrion, kidney beans.
  • Giant pandas spend 10-16 hours a day feeding, mainly on bamboo.
  • Although pandas are 99% vegetarian their digestive system is typical of a carnivore.

Intersting Facts About Flying-Cicada

  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.
  • Cicadas don’t sing at night, so don’t blame them if you can’t sleep.
  • Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes. Keep on the lookout.
  • They are about the size of a grain of rice when they drop from a tree branch to the ground and start digging.
